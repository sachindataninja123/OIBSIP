Tribute To A.P.J Abdul Kalam

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man Of India

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam , also known as India's Missile Man, was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he specialized in physics and aeronautical engineering. Kalam spent four decades as a scientist and science administrator at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), playing a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He was instrumental in the development of India's nuclear and missile programs, including the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. After his presidency, Kalam resumed civilian life, engaging in teaching, writing, and public service. Some of his notable works include "Wings of Fire," "India 2020 - A Vision for the New Millennium," "My Journey," and "Ignited Minds - Unleashing the Power Within India," which have become household names in India and among Indian nationals abroad.

Biographies

  • He began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • He led the project to develop India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully launched the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • He played a significant role in the development of India's missile programs, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • He also contributed to the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which helped establish India as a nuclear power.
  • He served as the Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister in the 1990s before becoming President in 2002.
